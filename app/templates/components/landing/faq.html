Operational Briefing (FAQ)

Everything you need to know before deployment.

Is this for beginners or advanced engineers?
Securithon Lab is designed for Intermediate to Advanced engineers. We assume you know how to write code and use a terminal. Our scenarios range from mid-level logic flaws to highly advanced infrastructure misconfigurations.
Do I need to install VMs or Docker locally?
No. Securithon Lab provides Zero-Setup Cloud Workspaces. When you initialize a track, we spin up isolated, dedicated containers (Postgres, Redis, APIs) directly in the cloud. You access everything through our in-browser IDE and terminal.
What programming languages are supported?
Currently, our AppSec tracks heavily feature Python (FastAPI/Django) and Node.js (Express/NestJS). Our InfraSec tracks require knowledge of Bash, Nginx, and YAML (Kubernetes/Docker-Compose).
How does the Automated Verification work?
When you submit a patch, our proprietary backend compiles your code and runs a suite of automated exploits (DAST) against your running container. If our exploits fail and your unit tests pass, the vulnerability is considered patched.
